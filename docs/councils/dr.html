COUNCIL BACKGROUND

The Dewan Rakyat, literally meaning the House of Representatives, acts as Malaysia's lower house of its federal legislature. The Dewan Rakyat is a directly elected body, with its 222 Members of Parliament being elected with the single-winner voting rule. It is responsible for the passing and amending of the primary legislation, otherwise known as Acts of Parliaments.

Taking place in post-independence Malaysia, the Dewan Rakyat explores ways the newly founded nation can develop. Be it through economic reforms or infrastructural development, the council explores pivotal decisions that will determine Malaysia's future, greatly aligning with SDG 9.
